## **The Major Arcana: Pre- & Post-Immortality**

### **1. The Fool (You, The Player)**

- **Pre-Time Skip:** A rebellious figure, the only one actively trying to prevent the Arcana from attaining immortality. Close friends with The Magician and childhood sweetheart to The World. Their motivations stem from an understanding that people are not meant to live forever.
- **Post-Rune Magic:** Put into a 10,000-year sleep, awakening unchanged while all others have decayed mentally and emotionally. They are _the only one resistant to immortality’s side effects_. Slowly uncovering their own past and questioning their innocence.

### **2. The Magician (The Fool’s Best Friend)**

- **Pre-Time Skip:** A prodigious mind, always seeking knowledge and control over their fate. They admired The Fool’s ideals but ultimately feared death too much to resist immortality.
- **Post-Rune Magic:** Driven insane by time, The Magician has lost themselves in an endless pursuit of mastery. Initially unrecognizable to The Fool, they may speak in riddles or fragments of past memories. Despite their madness, they _still_ believe The Fool was right all along.

### **3. The High Priestess**

- **Pre-Time Skip:** A scholar and keeper of knowledge, trusted to safeguard ancient secrets. They were cautious about the rune magic but ultimately allowed it.
- **Post-Rune Magic:** Has withdrawn entirely into secrecy, refusing to acknowledge the outside world. Holds knowledge of the past but will only reveal it cryptically, if at all.

### **4. The Empress**

- **Pre-Time Skip:** A kind and nurturing leader, deeply empathetic. Wanted immortality to continue caring for their people.
- **Post-Rune Magic:** A twisted motherly figure, overprotective to the point of suffocation. Keeps an eternal, stagnant "family" that never changes, forcing loyalty through fear and dependence.

### **5. The Emperor**

- **Pre-Time Skip:** A firm but just ruler, always striving for order and stability.
- **Post-Rune Magic:** Has become a tyrant who enforces rigid control over their domain. Believes change is weakness and violently suppresses it.

### **6. The Hierophant**

- **Pre-Time Skip:** A religious figure, guiding others in spiritual matters.
- **Post-Rune Magic:** Now a zealot who worships immortality itself, convincing others that their unchanging nature is divine will.

### **7. The Lovers**

- **Pre-Time Skip:** Two inseparable souls, deeply in love.
- **Post-Rune Magic:** Their love has decayed into an obsession. They remain physically together but have lost all emotional connection, trapped in an endless cycle of trying to rekindle something long dead.

### **8. The Chariot**

- **Pre-Time Skip:** A warrior, determined and focused on achieving their goals.
- **Post-Rune Magic:** Now unable to _stop_ moving forward. They are an eternal conqueror, always chasing new battles even when there are none left to fight.

### **9. Strength**

- **Pre-Time Skip:** A figure of quiet resilience, known for their gentle strength.
- **Post-Rune Magic:** Their patience has eroded, turning them into a brute who crushes all opposition rather than guiding others.

### **10. The Hermit**

- **Pre-Time Skip:** A wise, reclusive scholar.
- **Post-Rune Magic:** The last truly reflective being among the immortals. They have spent eternity observing how the Arcana fell into their worst selves and now act as a guide to The Fool, explaining their descent.

### **11. Wheel of Fortune**

- **Pre-Time Skip:** A believer in fate and the natural flow of time.
- **Post-Rune Magic:** A broken gambler, constantly trying to "force" luck. Obsessed with controlling chance, as eternity has stripped them of the natural flow of change.

### **12. Justice**

- **Pre-Time Skip:** A fair and impartial judge.
- **Post-Rune Magic:** Now overly harsh, seeing all things as black and white. Punishes even the smallest deviation from "the way things have always been."

### **13. The Hanged Man**

- **Pre-Time Skip:** A philosopher who sought understanding through self-sacrifice.
- **Post-Rune Magic:** Has become apathetic, hanging in a state of eternal limbo, refusing to act or interfere in anything.

### **14. Death**

- **Pre-Time Skip:** A force of natural endings and transformation.
- **Post-Rune Magic:** Now stripped of their purpose, they seek destruction as a desperate attempt to create change. They are feared but secretly _long_ for something to finally end them.

### **15. The Devil**

- **Pre-Time Skip:** A charismatic figure, indulging in pleasures but never losing themselves completely.
- **Post-Rune Magic:** Now an embodiment of addiction and excess. They trap others in endless loops of pleasure and indulgence to avoid confronting the meaninglessness of eternity.

### **16. The Tower**

- **Pre-Time Skip:** A revolutionary, always challenging the status quo.
- **Post-Rune Magic:** Eternity has stripped them of their purpose. They have become an unstable, destructive force, randomly tearing down structures out of boredom rather than necessity.

### **17. The Star**

- **Pre-Time Skip:** An optimist and healer, always seeing the good in the world.
- **Post-Rune Magic:** Now lost in delusion, trying to pretend that nothing has changed. They refuse to acknowledge the stagnation around them.

### **18. The Moon**

- **Pre-Time Skip:** A dreamer, an artist, someone deeply in tune with the subconscious.
- **Post-Rune Magic:** Now drowning in illusions, unable to distinguish reality from their own warped perceptions.

### **19. The Sun**

- **Pre-Time Skip:** A joyful and warm person, spreading happiness.
- **Post-Rune Magic:** Now aggressively forcing others to be "happy," punishing sadness or doubt. Their world is a false paradise where suffering is ignored.

### **20. Judgement (The World’s Half-Sibling)**

- **Pre-Time Skip:** A protector, always guiding others toward their destiny.
- **Post-Rune Magic:** Now the enforcer of stagnation. They see change as dangerous and act as the final judge of those who defy the way things "should" be.

### **21. The World (The Fool’s Childhood Sweetheart)**

- **Pre-Time Skip:** A seeker of knowledge, wanting to understand everything.
- **Post-Rune Magic:** The ultimate embodiment of stagnation. She _completed_ her journey by attaining all four runes, but in doing so, lost the ability to move forward. Trapped in the past, she tries to keep things as they were—forgetting The Fool in the process.
