The **Rot spreads,** creeping through stone, soil, and sky alike. It **does not kill—it consumes,** claiming bodies, minds, and wills, leaving only empty husks that wander in service of something unknowable. The infection is slow, mercifully avoidable with distance, and even reversible—**so long as it has not fully taken hold.** But should one linger too long… they cease to be themselves.

Here, in the **Grand West**, civilization clings to existence. This vast land remains untouched by the Rot, a final refuge for those who refuse to surrender. Yet, for all its safety, the Grand West is **not kind.** It is barren of old civilizations, known for **driving travelers mad or simply leading them astray.** It is a land of caves, of eerie beauty, of whispered myths—most notably, the **Old Tarot Clan**, the supposed namesake of those who now fight to hold the line.

General Ernest is one such warrior, his camp standing as one of the last outposts of **order before the unknown.**

### **The Land and Its Strange Bounty**

The camp sits at the **mouth of a cavern**, the resting place of the **Heart Gem**, one of the four Runes of power. The land here is unnatural:

- **Crimson grass fields**, shifting and pulsing as though alive.
    
- **Heart-beast stags**, their glasslike bodies revealing a still-beating heart within.
    
- **Hand-things**, scuttling creatures like disembodied fingers, crawling toward something unseen.
    
- **Breathing roses**, their petals parting and closing as if sighing in relief—or mourning.
    

Yet **this land sustains its people**.

- **Farms** provide carefully cultivated food, protected from the Rot’s creeping grasp.
    
- **Gemfruits**, crystalline and vibrant, grow wild across the Grand West, a rare gift in this land of scarcity.
    

For now, **supplies hold**, but survival is never guaranteed.

### **The Camp and Its People**

The **camp houses nearly 100 souls**, a fragile coalition of those who refuse to give in. The Rot has forced **unlikely alliances**, as old divisions matter less than survival.

#### **The Races of the Grand West**

- **Humans, Elves, and Orcs**: The backbone of the camp, each carrying their own burdens and histories.
    
- **Dwarves and Goblins**: Forced to abandon old grudges, now working together in the deep caves.
    
- **Airfolk (Harpies, Aarakocra, and others)**: Once untouchable in their high perches, now forced to land, for even the skies are no longer safe.
    
- **Heart-Touched**: Those forever changed by the Heart Gem, their eyes red-tinted, their veins pulsing with unnatural rhythm.
    

And then there are the **Rune-born races**, those created or changed by the four great Runes themselves.

- **The Diamond-Kin ("Geos")**: Towering, dense beings of stone and crystal, their bodies slow but unyielding. **They are builders, defenders—gentle giants unless forced to act.**
    
- **The Cloverborn ("Verdans")**: Quick, erratic plant-like creatures, more instinct than intellect. They move with **impossible luck**, dodging attacks that should have struck true, surviving where others would perish.
    
- **The SpadeBots ("Remnans")**: Sentient machines with a strange fate. Each one lives a full life—until their inevitable **Update**, where their mind wipes clean, leaving behind only their body. **To them, each existence is but a chapter in a longer story.**
    

**For now, these races coexist**, bound by necessity. But tensions lurk beneath the surface, and some wonder whether the Rune-born should be trusted at all.

### **The Girl Who Has Touched Three Runes**

At the center of the camp’s greatest conflict lies **a single girl.**

She sleeps within a guarded tent, untouched by the madness that should have taken her. **She has touched three Runes**—a feat that should have shattered her mind, left her a screaming, mindless wretch like all the others who came before.

But she remains whole.

She is **General Ernest’s own blood**, and her fate is the camp’s greatest debate.

- **To some, she is a beacon of hope**—proof that the Runes can be harnessed without losing oneself.
    
- **To others, she is a threat**—a ticking time bomb, her mind simply waiting to snap.
    
- **And then there are those who whisper that she is already awake.**
    

### **The Road Ahead**

This camp is one of **several outposts** within the Grand West, though **Ernest’s relationships with the other military leaders are strained**. The **greater Tarot Clan** fights for order, but not all agree on how it should be achieved.

For now, the **Rot remains distant, last seen hundreds of miles away.** But all know that distance is an illusion.

**You have come to this camp for your own reasons.**

- Are you a **warrior**, seeking to hold back the tide?
    
- A **scholar**, desperate to unlock the secrets of the Runes?
    
- A **wanderer**, drawn by fate or something deeper?
    
- Or something else entirely?
    

### **Where Do You Stand?**

- **The Runes**: Salvation, or a curse waiting to consume all?
    
- **The Girl**: A miracle to be awakened, or a danger to be kept asleep?
    
- **The Grand West**: A last refuge, or the calm before the storm?
    

**Welcome to the last stand of the Tarot Clan. The choice is yours.**
