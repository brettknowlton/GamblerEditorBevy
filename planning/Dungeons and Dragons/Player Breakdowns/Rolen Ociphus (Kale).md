<H4>Background</H4>
Rolen's father, Callon Living Blade, was one of the most renowned swordsmen in his time, a highly charismatic fighter. A legend, Callon later in his life settled down and started a family. Rolen grew up very wealthy and grew accustomed to that lifestyle. Both Rolen and his brother Varus, loved and idolized their father. They wanted to be just like him. Rolen would spend hours reading about his fathers deeds and talking to his father about them. Rolen loved learning the strategy involved. Callon taught that defeating your oppenent took two parts, knowing and reading them and swinging a blade. 

  

Rolen struggled to learn the blade, with years and years of effort and very little to show for it. Rolen learned and excelled in strategy and learning and Varus excelled in the blade.

  

Rolen saw much greater value in the power of knowledge than learning the blade. The day he quit being a disciple of the Living Blade, his father cut him on his cheek to teach Rolen that his life is forever in his own hands. If he can't rely on himself for protection, he's already dead. Rolen forever remembers this day with mixed feelings, gratitude for his life sparred, relief of not having to follow his fathers footsteps, fear of not being able to protect his loved ones, and shame for letting his father down.

  

Rolen instead took after his mother as a musician. However, Rolen's first love was learning—he played music and told bouts of lore at libraries, universities, and temples of learning, just so he places to learn of knowledge and inspiring tales.

  

Rolen and his brother [[Varus Ociphus]] were almost of age to be men, when their father was murdered. Rolen was there when a black clad figure surprised attacked Callon. Paralyzed with fear, Rolen watched helplessly as his father was killed in cold blood. Rolen and his family were devastated and the murderer was unknown. Rolen took two vows that day, one to find the murderer of his father and two, to always look after Varus. 

  

At this time, Rolens mother gifted him a silver rose necklace that was hidden as a pan flute.

  

All the while, Rolen grew up with his best friend Morzan, their bond strengthened by their shared loss of parents; Morzan lost both of his parents to the Red Cult. This spurred Morzan's desire to become an Archfeys inquisitor like his parents, and studied magicical truths. After traveling in the forest to find an ancient hidden library, Rolen and Morzan were attacked by a manticore. Rolen was gravely injured, losing his left arm and nearly dying. Morzan managed to scare off the satisfied manticore and saved Rolen's life by making a deal with an Archfey, healing Rolen with an arm of the forest. This experience further bonded Rolen and Morzan.

  

In the pursuit of finding his fathers murderer, Rolen solved a few crimes of his own. He found his true passion of making the world a better place by solving mysteries, bringing down bad guys, and bringing the truth to the light. Between his love of learning, playing music like his mother, and solving crimes, Morzan was right there along side him. These two hoped one day to be inquisitors for an Archfey.

  

However, Varus turned to crime. Small thefts at first slowly escalated to greater crimes. Rolen warned against Varus's crimes but loved his brother. Varus started as a mercenary for the Red Cult, making him a supporter, which was punishable by death. When Rolen found out about Varus's most recent dealings, he was scared of the repercussions of taking down his brother and waited. Rolen confronted Varus and convinced him to quit, but it was too late.

  

Morzan, an inquisitors apprentice, like his parents, discovered Varus's heinous crime as well. Rolen subtly confronts Morzan to convince him to leave Varus alone. Morzan refuses and apologizes to Rolen.

  

Rolen watches his best friend, who's saved his life, walk away to give his brother a death sentence. A thought flashes across Rolens mind, 'I can't lose Varus like I did my father.'

  

Rolen, tears starting to stream down his scarred cheek, calls out. "Morzan, I'm sorry it had end this way, I really am.... Thanks for everything." Morzan turns around curiously as Rolen casts Dissanont Whispers on his best friend. Morzan dies trying to crawl away from Rolen as he cradles his best friend in his arms.

  

If Rolen acted sooner when he first discovered Varus's involvement with the Red Cult, this never would've happened.

  

Rolen and Varus realized they had to run. They said goodbye to their mother and said they'd come home one day and fled into the material plane. Shortly after, Rolen woke up to find Varus gone along with all of his valuables. Rolen sought to fulfill the promise to his mother to take care of Varus, all the while his forest arm reminded him of the life given and the one taken.
