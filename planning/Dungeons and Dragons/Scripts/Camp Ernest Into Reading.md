
Nestled against the western face of the mountain, Camp Ernest stands as a beacon of structure in a world steadily unraveling. To the east, far beyond sight but never beyond thought, lies the **Rotten Continent**—a land devoured by the creeping plague that twists flesh, steel, and stone alike. The mountains serve as the final natural barricade, shielding what remains of the **Grand West** from the encroaching madness.

On the other side of these towering peaks lies the stronghold of **Helena Shinstar**, an Aasimar of cold efficiency, commanding an outpost built like a fortress. Unlike Ernest’s camp, which balances order with reluctant hospitality, Helena’s domain is **a wall in both purpose and spirit**, a place where only the strongest or most fortunate earn a place within its defenses.

The Grand West itself is a vast, untamed land where survival demands either numbers or cunning. Some form **temporary camps**, others wander as **Nomadic Clans**, seeking fortune, safety, or a means to outlast the inevitable spread of the Rot. Here, history is more myth than memory—tales of the **Old Tarot Clan**, an ancient people said to have ruled these lands long before the world began to crumble. They were powerful, they were wise… and they vanished, leaving not a single trace. Most dismiss them as little more than campfire stories, cautionary tales about the dangers of knowledge too vast for mortal minds.

But for now, history is of little concern.

### **A Refugee Surge and a Census in Chaos**

The latest influx of refugees has brought the camp to its limits. It isn’t uncommon for the Rot to push survivors westward, but **this wave is massive**, overwhelming the camp’s already strained resources. **General Arthur Ernest**—a man of rigid principle—has **ordered a full restructuring**:

- **All members must re-enlist, identify, and be accounted for.**
    
- **Temporary work assignments are being issued until a full census is complete.**
    
- **New arrivals must submit to scrutiny, regardless of their history.**
    

For most, this is nothing more than another layer of bureaucracy, a small price to pay for continued safety. But for a few, this is a dangerous complication.

### **Two Young Travelers and a Dangerous Deal**

Among the refugees stand two figures who do not quite belong.

- **Rolen Ociphus**, a Halfling outcast, exiled for **troublemaking, defiance, and survival at any cost.** He should have perished in the wilderness. Instead, he thrived.
    
- **Lucas [Unknown]**, a bard whose **past is riddled with betrayals both given and received**. His secrets run dark, and his songs, darker still.
    

Bound by a deal made in the depths of the wilds, they have traveled together across the Grand West. Their knowledge of the land is evident. Their lack of Rot symptoms is suspicious.

Now, as they step into **Camp Ernest**, they find themselves in an uncomfortable position. To stay means to **blend in, to play the part of refugees seeking safety.** To stand out means **risking questions they are not prepared to answer.**

The census is coming. Their identities will be tested. **And somewhere beyond the mountain, the Rot waits.**
