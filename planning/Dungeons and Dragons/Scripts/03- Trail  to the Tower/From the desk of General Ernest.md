To the Lady Helena Shinstar,  
Matriarch of the Tower

From the desk of General Ernest  

---
Sealed with urgency. Delivered by trusted hand.
Lady Helena,

I write not out of formality nor diplomacy, but necessity. The bloodwheat has moved — not grown, not spread, but moved — and in doing so, has made clear what I feared: this is not simply corruption. This is intelligence.

Our fields are ruptured. Our stores have burned. And in the chaos of retreat, I have witnessed things my men cannot explain, and I will not waste your time with speculation. You are too old for that. We both are.

I am sending this letter in haste because I do not know the direction this threat may take next, only that your silence on the matter is deafening. If I find any trace that this… infestation found footing under your watch, or worse, by your hand, I will act without restraint to preserve what is left of our home.

But if this threat is what I suspect — a mimicry of the Rot’s spread but of wholly different nature — then none of us are immune, not even you and your tower of cold stone and clearer thought.

This is not a warning of retaliation. This is a plea wrapped in iron. If you are innocent of this, and I sincerely hope you are, then prepare your walls. Look to your people. Ready your mind for the unthinkable.

I do not ask for alliance. Only truth.  
Do not make me come for it myself.

— A. E.  
General of Camp Ernest  
Warden of the Southern Front**
