## 🎬 **SESSION TITLE: “Trail to the Tower”**

---

## 🟢 **PREP: Session Start (10–15 min)**

- Reintroduce the party: Rolen, Vesta, Verity, GibGab, Melborne
    
- Setting: Morning. Party is leaving the base of the valley guiding a wagon pulled by Geos en route to “The Tower.”
    
- Describe landscape: red valley with distant stone peaks
    

**Remind them:**

- The canyon is half a day away.
    
- Weather is calm… for now.
    

---

## 🟡 **ACT 1: "A Cart and a Cause" (45–60 min)**

### ✅ Required Moments:

1. 🎒 **Michael Scandopa discovered** in the cart.
    
2. 🏹 **Lucas confrontation** — animal-handling check determines number of wolves (d20 ÷ 3, rounded up). max 5
    
3. 🐕 **Melborne's wolf pup revealed**.
    

---

### 🎲 Key Beats:

- Roleplay who drives/walks.
    
- Michael tries to stay hidden for a bit — let GibGab or someone notice something weird in the cart.
    
- Lucas attack: choose canyon treeline or ridge ledge ambush.
    
- Roleplay-heavy: Rolen calming Lucas. Other players may want to engage wolves or persuade.
    
- Wolf pup reveal can defuse the moment — it’s adorable.
    

---

### 🧠 Contingency Plans:

- 🛑 **Players refuse to engage with Michael** — he follows behind awkwardly, tripping or offering weird advice until acknowledged.
    
- 🏃 **Refuse to ride cart** — a muddy patch, thorns, or steep terrain make walking unviable.  
    “The cart’s the only way through this stretch.”
    
- 🎯 **Lucas goes full aggression** — wolves target weapons/hands, not necks. Use **non-lethal intent** language: “They’re trying to _push you away_, not rip you apart.”
    

---

### ⏳ Time Extenders (if party breezes through):

- A short encounter with a traveling herbalist or lost mapmaker NPC.
    
- One of the wolves is visibly rotting — tie-in to future Rot themes.
    

---

## 🔵 **ACT 2: "Smoke on the Mountain" (60–75 min)**

### ✅ Required Moments:

1. 🔥 **Camp set-up** in an abandoned firepit area.
    
2. 🐻 **Nighttime corrupted bear attack** (hard but winnable).
    
3. 🗣️ **Fireside interrogation of Michael** — heavy hints at celestial ancestry.
    
4. 🕊️ **Altar with celestial script** — tithe decision made in morning.
    

---

### 🎲 Key Beats:

- Players search for firewood? Discover it in the cart.
    
- Let them choose watches (great time for flavor).
    
- Describe the bear: fur sloughing, sick aggression.
    
- Michael's talk is prompted naturally by a quiet night or player suspicion.
    

**If they tithe ≥10gp at altar → No flood. If not → Act 3 gets _wet_.**

---

### 🧠 Contingency Plans:

- ⚔️ **Party wipes in bear fight?** Make the bear a diseased mother who flees at 5 HP or if her lair is threatened.
    
- 🤐 **Players ignore Michael** — have him talk in his sleep about “the lake” or “a mother with silver eyes.”
    
- 🔎 **Nobody reads Celestial?** Michael “feels” something, and Lucas recognizes it vaguely — allow **Arcana or Insight** check to learn the altar's purpose.
    

---

### ⏳ Time Extenders:

- NPCs on road with Rot-infected companion (Verity or Vesta could spot symptoms).
    
- A dying bird lands at their feet during watch — its mouth opens and whispers in Celestial. Vanishes into dust.
    

---

## 🔴 **ACT 3: “The Fall and the Flood” (60–75 min)**

### ✅ Required Moments:

1. 🐂 **Geos begin ignoring commands** at canyon mouth.
    
2. 🪨 **A player is pushed — the wolf pup falls.**
    
3. 🕳️ **Melborne leaps after it, lets go of another's hand**.
    
4. 🌊 **Flood hits (if they didn’t tithe)**.
    
5. 🧠 **Geos move as if commanded by Helena**.
    

---

### 🎲 Key Beats:

- Make the narrowing canyon feel **tense and alive**.
    
- Player falls near ledge — Melborne’s moment of choice.
    
- Use **slow motion narration** as he lets go.
    
- The water event (if triggered) can be a brief skill challenge (3 successes before 2 fails).
    
- End on Helena's **subtle influence** and Geos pulling the cart toward a glowing point.
    

---

### 🧠 Contingency Plans:

- ❓ **Players hesitate to let Melborne act** — someone else’s stumble causes the pup to fall anyway.
    
- 🚱 **No flood but you want drama?** Canyon quakes. Rockslide seals way back. One Geo nearly goes over.
    
- 🗣️ **They try to call back the Geos** — no response. Insight reveals: “They are not listening to _you_ anymore.”
    

---

### 🎬 Session Close

Final scene:

> _As the Geos continue their climb up the canyon wall trail, the group watches the light of the tower flicker into view through the mist — golden, crystalline, and distant. A silence lingers among the party — loss, awe, or both._
