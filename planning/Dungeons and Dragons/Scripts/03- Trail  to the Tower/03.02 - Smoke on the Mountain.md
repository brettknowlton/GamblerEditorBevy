> _"The canyon begins to darken as the sun lowers behind the cliffs. A gentle wind slides through the narrow pass, bringing a chill that wasn’t there before. Up ahead: a small campground — abandoned, but strangely clean. A circle of stones suggests a recent fire, long since snuffed."_

---

### 🛑 **Scene 1: Setting Camp**

**Details**:

- Abandoned fire pit, still soot-marked.
    
- Broken crate near the side (empty, but marked “Ern. Provisions”)
    
- There _is_ dry firewood buried in the wagon's underside storage (Perception or Investigation DC 10).
    

**Prompt the players**:

- “Who starts the fire?”
    
- “Where does everyone set up for the night?”
    
- “Anyone take first watch?” _(tee this up for the fight coming)_

### 🧠 **Scene 3: Fireside Conversations**

> The fire crackles again. Smoke filters through the pine. Wounds bandaged, pulses still high. Michael stares into the flames.

Give them **space to decompress** and talk.

---

#### 🕯️ **Scandopa Interrogation (Required)**

**Prompted by someone asking "Where did you learn that?" or “You don’t seem like just some stowaway.”**

**Scandopa’s dialogue direction**:

> “My ma used to sing to me in a language I didn’t understand. I asked her what it was once — she just smiled and said ‘Ask the lake.’ Never knew what she meant.”

> “She said I was _touched_ — not in the head, I mean — she said the stars sang louder when I cried as a baby. Weird stuff. You hear that, and then people start looking at you funny. So I left.”

- He’s **not Aasimar**, but **half-celestial by bloodline**.
    
- The party _may believe_ he is Helena’s son, but he’s actually child of **The Lover** — this will resonate if any players know Arcana lore.
    

> “Ernest gave me this once.” _(pulls out a pendant with military-style etching. Initials G.E.)_  
> “Said I’d always have a place if I needed it. But I don’t think I do.”

📌 **Player Insight**:

- Allow Insight check DC 14: “He’s _absolutely_ hiding something bigger — but the fear in his eyes is real.”
    

---

### 🌧️ **Scene 4: The Altar and the Choice (Dawn)**

> As the sun rises, rainclouds begin forming fast. On a rock outcropping just ahead — an old **marble altar** barely visible among weeds.

**Inscribed in Celestial**:

> “Tithe unto the Lady of the Lake, and dry shall be your passage.”

Only those who read **Celestial** (possibly Vesta or Verity) can understand this.

If none do:

- **Lucas** may vaguely recognize it: “That’s angel script, isn’t it?”
    
- **Michael** stares but says: “Don’t know the words. But I feel… something. Like it’s waiting.”
    

---
