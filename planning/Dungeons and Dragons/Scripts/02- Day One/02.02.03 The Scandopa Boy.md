## 🧨 **Foreshadow Moment – During Bloodwheat Golem Attack**

Use this **after** a player does something brave or is about to get hurt.

> _(The chaos of the battlefield is overwhelming. Screams, snapping wheat stalks, and the grinding of golem limbs. A refugee trips and falls, just as a golem rears back to strike.)_

Suddenly—

> _The air **distorts with a shimmer**, like heatwaves off metal. The golem’s fist punches through nothing—its target is gone._  
> _Ten feet away, the refugee appears—dazed, unharmed._  
> _You catch the scent of burning paper and cloves._  
> _A faint, echoing voice rings out across the chaos, as if from behind a curtain:_  
> **“You’re welcome!”**

> _(Anyone who looks toward the voice sees only the shimmer of a shadow ducking around a tent flap, gone before you can place a face.)_

> _Later, someone murmurs near the triage tent:_  
> **“That had to be Scandopa again… freakin’ illusion kids...”**

## 🕯 **Nighttime Roleplay – Michael’s First Contact**

Choose **any party member** who’s alone, thoughtful, or feeling off-guard. This works great with Vesta, Rolen, or even GibGab. Here’s the setup:

> _(The camp has quieted. Distant fires crackle. The stars are blurred by the faint haze of smoke and dust. You sit alone—maybe cleaning a weapon, mending clothes, or staring out toward the forest.)_

> _From behind you, a voice says softly:_  
> **“You did good out there.”**

> _(You turn to see a **young Tiefling** leaning against a crooked wooden post. He’s probably 17, with bright eyes and a sly grin. One horn is chipped. His cloak is patched, his sleeves are stained with ink and powdered chalk. His fingers are smudged with soot.)_

> **Michael:** “I saw how you moved. How you watched people. You’re not just some refugee... you _think_ like a player.”  
> _(He flicks a copper coin between his fingers—then it vanishes. You feel it land in your pocket.)_

> _(He doesn’t ask for your name. He already knows it.)_

> **Michael:** “This place won’t hold together. People keep trying to act like the Rot can be charted on a map. But it’s like... mold in the walls. You only notice it when something collapses.”  
> _(beat)_  
> **“But you—if you're smart—you can walk the mold before it cracks.”_

> _He steps away, pulling up his hood. The firelight catches a sigil painted faintly on his sleeve: a closed eye over a flame._

> **Michael (softly):** “We’ll talk again. When it’s time.”

> _(And just like that, he’s gone.)_
